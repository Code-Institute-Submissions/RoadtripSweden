Lofoten

Lofoten is an archipelago and a traditional district in the county of Nordland, Norway. Lofoten is known for a distinctive scenery with dramatic mountains and peaks, open sea and sheltered bays, beaches and untouched lands.

Its largest town, Leknes, lies approximately 169 km inside the Arctic Circle, or approximately 2,420 km away from the North Pole, thus making Lofoten one of the world's northernmost populated regions. Though lying within the Arctic Circle, the archipelago experiences one of the world's largest elevated temperature anomalies relative to its high latitude.
Lofoten is known for excellent fishing, nature attractions such as the northern lights and the midnight sun, and small villages off the beaten track. Kayak between the islands, go fishing for the catch of your life, or look for sea eagles soaring in the sky.

Lofoten is in the process of being certified as a Sustainable Destination, a seal of approval given to destinations that work systematically to reduce the negative impact of tourism. In addition to providing visitors with enjoyable experiences, Lofoten wishes to preserve the local nature, culture and environment, strengthen social values, and be economically viable. The municipality and the travel industry cooperate closely to assure that the destination is a great place both to live in and to visit.

ROUNDTRIP

If you want to make a really memorable longer road trip, you should drive from Stockholm to Norrland. Here are different routes to choose depending on what you want to see on your trip. One proposal is to drive from Stockholm along the coast to Umeå, Luleå and on towards Kiruna and Abisko. You can also drive through Östersund up towards Jokkmokk and further north. And why not continue across the Norwegian border to Narvik and Lofoten! The mountain landscape is beautiful all year round and there is a lot to explore along the way. If you go during the summer, you can experience the midnight sun far north and the northern lights in the winter.
